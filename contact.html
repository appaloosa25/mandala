Submission guidelines

Mandala welcomes original research articles (4,000–10,000 words), policy essays (1,500–4,000 words), and field reports (1,000–3,000 words). We accept work from researchers, practitioners, and students at any career stage.

All submissions should include: an abstract (150–200 words), five keywords, author biography (50–80 words), and any data availability statement. We do not require a specific citation style — use whichever is standard in your field, applied consistently.

We particularly welcome submissions from researchers based in cities that are underrepresented in the urban studies literature. If you are unsure whether your work is a fit for Mandala, send us a short pitch (200–300 words) before preparing a full submission.

Review timeline

After submission, manuscripts enter a desk review stage (1–2 weeks). Work that passes desk review is sent for double-blind peer review. We aim to provide first decisions within eight weeks of submission. We do not charge any fee at any stage of the process.
